0 1:1 The proverbs of Solomon the son of David, king of Israel; 
1 8:1 Who is as the wise man? and who knoweth the interpretation of a thing? a man’s wisdom maketh his face to shine, and the boldness of his face shall be changed. 
0 7:2 Keep my commandments, and live; and my law as the apple of thine eye. 
1 11:6 In the morning sow thy seed, and in the evening withhold not thine hand: for thou knowest not whether shall prosper, either this or that, or whether they both shall be alike good. 
0 15:30 The light of the eyes rejoiceth the heart: and a good report maketh the bones fat. 
1 7:5 It is better to hear the rebuke of the wise, than for a man to hear the song of fools. 
0 29:9 If a wise man contendeth with a foolish man, whether he rage or laugh, there is no rest. 
1 12:10 The preacher sought to find out acceptable words: and that which was written was upright, even words of truth. 
0 18:4 The words of a man’s mouth are as deep waters, and the wellspring of wisdom as a flowing brook. 
1 3:16 And moreover I saw under the sun the place of judgment, that wickedness was there; and the place of righteousness, that iniquity was there.

1 4:13 Better is a poor and a wise child than an old and foolish king, who will no more be admonished.

0 28:26 He that trusteth in his own heart is a fool: but whoso walketh wisely, he shall be delivered.
